Your bonus consists of the amount you kept from the $1 endowment (and did not invest) and the returns to the money you invested.

By investing, you can triple your bonus to up to $3. The returns to your investment depend on how much the worker's guess deviates from the actual number of dots.

The better the worker you invested in guessed, the more you will earn.


Your bonus in a round will be defined as:

Bonus = (Remaining) Endowment + Investment (in $) * 3 * (100 – relative deviation of the guess from the true value (in %))


To determine your bonus, we will randomly choose one of your decisions.

For example, if you invested all your endowment ($1) in this round, you will receive the following bonus:

  • If the accuracy of the worker’s guess deviates by 0% from the true value your bonus will be $3.
  • If the accuracy of the worker’s guess deviates by 50% from the true value your bonus will be $1.5.
  • If the accuracy of the worker’s guess deviates by 75% from the true value your bonus will be $0.75.
  • If the accuracy of the worker’s guess deviates by 100% from the true value your bonus will be $0.

You cannot receive a negative bonus. If the guess deviates by more than 100% of the actual value, the return to your investment will be 0.
